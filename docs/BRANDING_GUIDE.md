# Counter-Culture Branding Guide

> *How to tell, at a glance, whether a thing is Acid or Base — and why you'll
> probably need both.*

This guide defines **ecosystem roles**, not visual identity. For logo/color/type
rules see [`BRANDING.md`](./BRANDING.md). For the mathematical framing of the
two-chain thesis see [`PH_BALANCE_WHITEPAPER.md`](./PH_BALANCE_WHITEPAPER.md).

---

## Why this guide exists

When a developer, artist, or user arrives in web3, they are handed a hundred
chains and told "good luck." Most chains position themselves along a single
axis ("faster," "cheaper," "more scalable") and the actual *cultural* fit of
the chain — whether it's institutional or counterculture, suit or hoodie,
Coinbase or CBGB — is left for the user to figure out by vibes.

ACiD is explicit about its role so that:

- **Builders** know which chain to deploy which kind of app on
- **Users** know which chain matches their transaction intent
- **Partners** know which side of the ecosystem they're engaging
- **Content creators** know what "sounds like Acid" vs "sounds like Base"

The goal is not a war. The goal is a working ecosystem with both ends of
the pH scale covered.

## The Bridge — the relationship between them

ACiD does not hate Base. ACiD is not a "Base killer." ACiD is the other
end of the pH scale, and the scale needs both ends to mean anything.

```
 pH 1  ───────────  pH 7  ───────────  pH 14
ACiD                                   Base
 │                                       │
 └── counterculture, sovereign, velocity  └── institutional, compliant, custody
```

Healthy patterns between them:

- **Asset mobility.** Real value moves both ways over canonical bridges.
- **Narrative reciprocity.** Acid does not trash Base. Base does not pretend Acid doesn't exist.
- **Shared standards.** Both speak EVM. Both respect the OP Stack. Both are MIT-licensed.
- **User hat-switching.** The same human can be a Base user on payday and an Acid user on a Saturday night. That is the point.

Unhealthy patterns:

- "Which chain wins?" framing — this is a category error.
- Base pretending Acid doesn't exist — it does, and its users have capital.
- Acid LARPing as Base — compliance theater on a counterculture chain is the worst of both.

## Rules for contributors

If you're writing copy, designing art, shipping a feature, or running a
community channel **on ACiD**:

1. **Pick the role before the execution.** Is this Acid or could it live on Base? If the answer is "could live on Base," rewrite until it's specifically Acid.
2. **Never attack Base.** Rooting for them. Competing via contrast, not insult.
3. **Lowercase when in doubt.** Capitalization is for proper nouns and for `ACiD`.
4. **No rocket emojis. Ever.**
5. **Assume IYKYK.** Don't over-explain. The seeing eye is not captioned.
6. **If it feels like a press release, rewrite it.** If it feels like a note passed in class, you're close.
7. **Soul is not decoration.** A thing with soul has a reason every pixel is where it is. If you can't defend a choice, you didn't make a choice.

## Anti-patterns (do not ship)

- **Base cosplay.** Writing Acid copy in corporate voice to "seem serious." It reads as embarrassed about being Acid. Don't.
- **Edgelord overreach.** Trying too hard to be counterculture. Gratuitous profanity, anti-institutional chest-thumping, drug references without restraint. The guide says *psychedelic-restrained* for a reason — one drop, not a bucket.
- **Rainbow chaos.** Psychedelic does not mean every color at once. Acid green is the anchor. Secondary colors are accents, used sparingly.
- **Overpromising.** "Base killer," "10000x potential," "the future of X." If it could be in a rug-pull's Telegram, it can't be on an ACiD channel.
- **Mocking Base users.** The ETF-issuer who lives on Base is also a potential Acid user on the weekend. Don't alienate someone who is one mood shift from bridging over.
